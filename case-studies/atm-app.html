Overview

IRAVAAN is a comprehensive internal ATM money management system designed exclusively for One India ATM, a client who owns and operates ATMs across multiple branches. This internal application bridges the gap between ATM operators and loadmen (personnel who load cash into ATMs), providing real-time visibility into cash levels, denomination breakdowns, and loading history. The app is not available on public app stores as it serves as a private, enterprise-level solution for managing ATM operations and loadman coordination.

The Problem

ATM operators at One India ATM were facing critical operational challenges with manual money tracking:

No Real-Time Visibility

Operators had no instant way to check current money levels across multiple ATM branches.

Manual Calculation Errors

Loadmen manually calculated total amounts from denomination counts (₹500, ₹200, ₹100), leading to human errors.

Lack of Accountability

No transparent system to track who loaded money, when, and where.

Delayed Refill Response

Manual tracking led to delayed notifications when ATMs ran low on cash.

The Challenge

The challenge wasn't just digitizing the process—it was about creating an intuitive system that could handle complex data relationships while remaining simple enough for non-tech-savvy loadmen to use daily. I needed to:

Information Architecture

Design a clear hierarchy for managing multiple ATMs, loadmen, and denomination breakdowns without overwhelming users.

Error Prevention

Create an input system that eliminates manual calculation errors while maintaining speed.

Role-Based Design

Balance different user needs—admins need oversight, loadmen need quick data entry.

Real-Time Transparency

Build trust through instant visibility into all transactions and money movements.

Research & Insights

I conducted interviews with ATM operators and loadmen to understand their daily workflow and pain points. Here's what I discovered:

Dashboard Priority

Operators needed an at-a-glance view showing all ATMs and their status. Color-coded indicators were crucial for quick decision-making.

Speed Over Complexity

Loadmen work in time-sensitive environments. They needed to enter denomination counts in under 30 seconds with zero room for calculation errors.

Trust Through Transparency

Both parties needed to see the same data in real-time. Any delay or discrepancy would undermine trust in the system.

Proactive Notifications

Operators wanted to be alerted before ATMs ran critically low, not after customer complaints started coming in.

Key IA Decisions

  • → Separated admin and loadman interfaces completely to avoid confusion and feature bloat
  • → Kept navigation shallow (max 3 levels deep) for quick access to any feature
  • → Positioned denomination entry as a modal to maintain context and speed
  • → Made status indicators (Pending/Completed) visible at every level for transparency

The Solution

I designed a dual-interface system with distinct experiences for admins and loadmen, each optimized for their specific workflows.

For Admins

  • Dashboard showing all ATMs with real-time money levels
  • Create and manage loadmen for each ATM branch
  • Track which loadman loaded which ATM with timestamps
  • Receive push notifications when ATM money runs low

For Loadmen

  • Quick denomination entry with number steppers
  • Real-time auto-calculation of total amount
  • One-tap upload to system with instant confirmation
  • View assigned ATMs and loading history

UX Solutions Implemented

Visual Hierarchy Through Cards

Used card-based design to separate each ATM clearly. Color-coded status indicators (green = sufficient, yellow = low, red = critical) allowed admins to spot issues instantly without reading detailed numbers.

Smart Input Design

Implemented direct manual entry fields for denomination counts based on the bank's provided denomination list (₹500, ₹200, ₹100, etc.). As loadmen entered the number of notes for each denomination, the app calculated the total amount in real-time, eliminating calculation errors and reducing cognitive load.

Role-Based Navigation

Created separate navigation patterns for admins and loadmen. Admins saw overview metrics and management tools, while loadmen saw only their assigned ATMs and quick-entry forms.

Transparency Through Timeline

Added a timeline view showing who loaded money when, with denomination breakdowns. This created accountability and allowed admins to verify transactions at any time.

Proactive Notification System

Implemented threshold-based alerts that notified admins when cash levels dropped below configurable limits. Notifications included which ATM, current amount, and estimated hours until critical level.

Design System

A comprehensive design system built for consistency and scalability across the IRAVAAN ATM app.

Impact & Results

The app was developed by a colleague and deployed internally at One India ATM. The results exceeded expectations:

Instant Success

Immediate adoption by all loadmen and operators with zero onboarding friction. Users found the interface intuitive enough to use without training.

Zero Errors

No calculation errors or confusion reported post-launch. The auto-calculation and validation system eliminated the human error factor completely.

Operational Efficiency

Reduced money loading and verification time from 10+ minutes to under 1 minute. Real-time tracking eliminated follow-up calls and manual verification.

Enhanced Transparency

Complete visibility into all ATM operations built trust between operators and loadmen, reducing disputes and improving accountability.

Key Takeaways

Simplicity Wins in Operational Tools

The app's success came from ruthlessly simplifying complex operations. Every feature served a clear purpose—no bloat, no unnecessary steps.

Error Prevention > Error Correction

Instead of adding error correction flows, I designed input methods that made errors impossible. Constraints and auto-calculation eliminated the need for validation.

Real-Time Feedback Builds Trust

Showing instant calculations and immediate confirmations made users confident in the system. No waiting, no uncertainty—just immediate feedback.

Speed Matters in Time-Critical Apps

Designing for speed (3-day delivery) meant making smart trade-offs. I focused on core workflows that delivered maximum value, not peripheral features.
